JedEye

The JedEye is an Arduino-based handheld instrument designed for dry cave surveying. It measures azimuth (compass), clino, roll, and direct distance (lidar). A full user manual is published at https://sebkister.github.io/JedEye-Documentation/.

The device is operated through two on-device buttons labelled Next (move the menu cursor) and Select (activate), and is charged over USB-C.

Bluetooth pairing
The JedEye uses Bluetooth Low Energy only - there is no PIN-based pairing. To make it visible to TopoDroid, switch the device to its Distance Meter screen via the on-device menu. It then advertises as JedEye_<serial>.

In TopoDroid, open the Device screen and run BT Scan from the menu. The JedEye appears in the list; tap it to add it. Pairing only needs to be done once - subsequent connections are automatic when the device is back in the Distance Meter screen.

Taking a shot
On the Distance Meter screen the OLED shows the current lidar distance and the current heading and clino in real time. A small Bluetooth icon in the bottom-right corner indicates that BLE advertising is active.

To capture a measurement on the device, point it at the target and invoke Measure with the Next / Select buttons. The captured values are streamed to TopoDroid as a single shot. If TopoDroid's shot window is open, the new shot appears immediately in the list and is auto-assigned a station name like any other leg shot.

TopoDroid can also drive the JedEye remotely. While connected, tapping the Bluetooth button in the shot window opens a popup with laser on, laser off, shot, and device off options. Shot tells the JedEye to capture and send a measurement without touching the device - convenient when it is mounted on a tripod or otherwise out of reach.

Connection mode
The JedEye is always connected in continuous mode. Each measurement is transferred to TopoDroid as soon as it is taken. Shots taken while the device is not connected are not buffered or re-transmitted later - the JedEye is best used in sessions where it can remain connected to the Android throughout.

Battery
The JedEye runs on an internal battery and charges over USB-C. The current battery level is shown in the device's Info screen and as a small icon on the main display.
